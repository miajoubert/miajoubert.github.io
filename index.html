Who am I?

After working in tech support for 8 years, I began to notice that my favorite days were when I came across a new problem. No longer satisfied with "solving" the same problems over and over, I pursued software engineering to fulfill my desire for daily challenges.

Outside of work, you can find me reading fiction of all sorts, bending over a new puzzle on my kitchen counter, breezing through Sudokus, getting annoyed at the New York Times Crossword puzzles, or hiking trails across Wisconsin.

Why Software Engineering?

There was a distinct moment in my life when I realized that being a programmer was undeniably “cool.” While in the middle of playing Bot when I was about seven, I thought I had broken the computer when the game crashed. My father, who was a computer programmer, came to my rescue, and to my young eyes it seemed that his encoded instructions running through the computer was likely magic. The love I carried for understanding technology came to the surface as I started my first job out of college, ultimately leading me to an eight-year career in IT support. Nevertheless, I eventually ran out of daily challenges or sufficient problems to solve, instead applying the same solutions to similar problems on repeat. My unfulfillment illuminated my path toward becoming a programmer, giving me the opportunities to face new challenges each day and to apply my already-strong troubleshooting skills to debug code errors.

My journey to becoming a developer culminated in a 24-week program, during which I spent over 1000 hours strengthening my knowledge in Javascript, Python, React/Redux, HTML, CSS, and multiple relational database language formats. Over that time, I had the opportunity to work in an Agile environment with SCRUM methods and version control through GitHub, building four complete full-stack applications in four separate one- and two-week sprints. In the process, I completed a functional clone of the task-management application, Todoist, perfectly replicating their styling formats. I encourage you to check out my quick growth and strong abilities throughout my projects in the portfolio link above.

As exemplified in my six-month boot camp, learning new technologies and picking up new coding languages comes naturally for me. While the program was certainly challenging at times, I finally feel fully engaged in a perfectly demanding field, sharpening my technical abilities and satisfying my desire to continue solving new puzzles. My six years of tech support for Northwestern Mutual, along with my two years as a life underwriter, can bring a unique and undeniably valuable perspective to my role as a developer. I am certain that I would be a strong asset to your team, helping to build the software solutions for the company’s digital needs.
