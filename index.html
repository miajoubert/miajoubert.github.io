Who am I?

After working in tech support for 8 years, I began to notice that my favorite days were when I came across a new problem. No longer satisfied with "solving" the same problems over and over, I pursued software engineering to fulfill my desire for daily challenges.

Outside of work, you can find me reading fiction of all sorts, bending over a new puzzle on my kitchen counter, breezing through Sudokus, getting annoyed at the New York Times Crossword puzzles, or hiking trails across Wisconsin.

Why Software Engineering?

My time in tech support helped me recognize my love for solving complex problems and applying unique solutions to unusual issues. However, it wasn't until I realized how much time I spend on jigsaw puzzles or how easily I can become swept up with a logic problem or fulfilling a home-repair need without going to the store that I realized how much joy engineering could bring into my life. Software engineering has provided me the opportunities to face new challenges each day and to apply my strong troubleshooting skills to debug code errors.

My journey included a 24-week fullstack sotware engineering program, in which I became proficient with Javascript, Python, React/Redux, HTML, CSS, Express, Flask, Docker, and multiple relational database management systems. Over that time, I had the opportunity to work in an agile environment with scrum methods and version control through GitHub, completing four full-stack applications in four separate one- and two-week sprints. Check out my quick growth and strong abilities illustrated in those projects, below.

Attending a six-month software boot camp proves that training in new technologies and picking up new coding languages comes naturally for me. The process of learning and growing in a demanding field, while sharpening my technical abilities, satisfies my desire to continue solving new puzzles. My experience as a tech support analyst, along with my time as a life underwriter, can bring a valuable perspective to my role as a developer.
